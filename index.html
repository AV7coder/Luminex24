Hackathon is an exhilarating coding competition for high school students, where teams of two tackle six real-world coding challenges using Python. With an emphasis on problem-solving skills, participants navigate time constraints, balance speed with accuracy, and interpret hints to devise comprehensive solutions. The event fosters resilience and composure under pressure while enhancing analytical thinking & real-world communication skills.

RoboRun is an exciting competition for high school students where teams design and construct robots to navigate a challenging obstacle course. With an emphasis on innovation and problem-solving, participants gain hands-on experience in robotics and automation while fostering teamwork and collaboration skills. The competition encourages creativity and practical insights, as teams optimize their designs under pressure to complete the course in the shortest time possible.

Eureka is a prestigious event welcoming teams of students from grades 6-8 and 9-12 to exhibit their scientific ingenuity and problem-solving prowess. Participants assemble models or display boards representing their projects within a 45-minute window, presenting them to esteemed judges for evaluation. Encouraging innovation and safety, Eureka promotes scientific exploration while cultivating essential skills such as critical thinking and communication.

Gaming is a competitive event catering to students in grades 6-12, featuring standard 5V5 Valorant matches. The competition follows a bracket format, with each match lasting 40 minutes. The victor is determined by the team with the most wins. The primary objective of this event is to cultivate essential skills in participating students, such as motor skills, cognitive thinking, team planning, coordination, and strategy development. This electrfying event await your participation.

Through GameGenesis, students not only learn coding basics but also nurture creativity while addressing real-world issues outlined by the SDGs.
